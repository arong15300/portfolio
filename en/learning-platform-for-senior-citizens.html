LEARNING PLATFORM FOR SENIOR CITIZENS

Web design UX/UI Research & Design | 2022

Overview

做伙動, learning platform established for Chang Gung Health Culture Village which won the Symbol of National Quality mark in 2021. We hope it can encourage senior citizens to participate in activities that will improve their physical and mental health,abilities of the senior citizens, while also reducing the burden on health care providers and providing health data tracking. We hope that after some time, if the platform proves successful, it can be implemented in society and provide a national registration system for senior citizens to indirectly improve their health and reduce their pain from diseases.

My role

Competitive Analysis, User interview, Research, UI/UX Design (Linebot/Web), Promotional Video

Duration

September 2021 to May 2022 (9 months)

Teams

Designers (1), Design Professor (3), Engineer team (**), Cooperated Field (2)

User Problem

To improve user experience, I've categorized our users into two groups: the villagers who participate in the activities, and the health-carers who plan them. The villagers' average age is 70+, and many of them are not tech-savvy, which makes it challenging for them to navigate the platform. In the past, the platform lacked a sorting feature, and the activity information was fragmented, leading to difficulties in registration and searching. On the other hand, the health-carers used to manage and plan activities on paper, making it challenging to keep track of the data and analyze which activities to hold or optimize. This process was burdensome for them.

The Goal

I’d like to create a solution that solves users' problems. It comes down to the following three aspects:

  • Unified: Unify fragmented activity information across Health Village in familiar platform
  • Accessible: Users should be guided through their task goals
  • Data-based: If users have more questions, they can reach out for the answer

Design Highlight

Personalized Recommendations Help Make Decisions

By empathizing with users, we understand that the seniors are not good at using the Internet making it difficult for them to search. Therefore, we have designed Personalized Activity Recommendations and analyzed the users' past activity records to provide activities that are most suitable and interesting for them. This helps meet user needs, achieve design goals, and increase activity participation rates.

Information list for basis of recommend

Design Highlight

The Right Platform Uses More Smoothly

We've discovered that most platforms utilize web pages for basic functionality, while LINE APP's group feature is utilized for message communication. Further research indicates that: 60% of seniors over the age of 55 own a smartphone, with over 90% of them using the LINE APP. Additionally, after speaking with staff at the health care village, we found that over 70% of the seniors there own a smartphone, and nearly 100% of them use the LINE APP. It's also worth noting that the village seniors are more familiar with mobile phones than other seniors.

Design Highlight

Online Activity History Makes The Future Activity Better

In the past, activities were recorded on paper, making it difficult to review them later. Therefore, we created several online functions and address this issue through empathizing with users. These records provide health carers with a more solid foundation for planning future activities.

This is a brand-new project. We must understand the inner workings of the health village, current conditions and administrative knowledge, in order to plan effectively. Therefore, we decided to conduct user interviews targeting the staff of the activity department at the health village, so that we can collect a significant amount of valuable information at the minimum cost.

Find-Outs

  1. Challenges with Information Management: In most cases, paper-based records such as attendance records and activity content are used, making it difficult to organize and access information. As a result, health village staff often rely on less efficient methods to plan activities and may manually register villagers for activities, leading to an increased workload.
  2. The Wrong Platform: There are also some activities that use the online platform to register, but most of the villagers are not familiar with network, so they will ask staff to assist in registration. Some elders don’t even use the platform, so they call the counter directly and ask the staff to help them join the activity by using the system's backend. It makes the original convenience to become a burden instead.
  3. No Place to Remind: When there are changes in location or time for an activity, the news is announced on the official website. However, since the villagers don't frequently use the internet, they may miss the information and end up going to the wrong location. Therefore, a platform with push notifications or other forms of reminders should be considered to keep villagers informed of any changes in activity schedules.

Find-Outs

  1. Users desire all information in one place: The activities of the health village are spraed across multiple platforms, including the course platform, official website, bulletin board and classroom door. When users are looking for new fun, they require a unified platform that integrate all the information they need.

    2. Users don't like fragmented messages

  2. Users prefer using a familiar platform: Seniors tend to use a familiar platform, so they don't need to pay extra time to learn new knowledge.
  3. Users prefer a simplified workflow: This is because seniors are not used to typing, easily get lost in the journey, and are not good at the Internet.
  4. Users seek guidance: When they lost in the platform, they prefer to have a guidance that can help them achieve their goals, rather than having to repeatedly seek help from others.
  5. Users want to track their activities: Activity need to be recorded to avoid forgetting. For the villagers, they desire someone can remind them to participate in the activity; For the staff, recording the seniors' activity process is valuable for analysis.

    6. Users desire to track and be reminded

Takeaway

The first whole project

Unlike designing a specific product function, my graduation project involves understanding the entire product journey, including the health village, the seniors, the stakeholders, and even my own role. In other words, it's both my graduation project and a business project. I am tasked with generating unique ideas that align with the practical requirements for actual implementation. Therefore, it is inevitable that some interesting ideas may be discarded. My goal is to strike a balance between uniqueness and consistency.

The challenges of designing at scale

In the past, I used to work on school projects either on my own or with classmates as a designer. However, this time, I collaborated with a complete team. Unlike previous experience, collaborative design comes with its own set of constraints, including considerations such as the client's brand concept, the preferences of stakeholders, and the technical limitations of the engineering team. It takes a lot of time and effort to understand their requirements and reach alignment. Additionally, we encountered unexpected challenges during the development process, which necessitated a thorough product redesign. Despite the thrilling, it ultimately resulted in a perfect ending.

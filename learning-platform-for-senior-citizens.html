LEARNING PLATFORM FOR SENIOR CITIZENS

Web design UX/UI Research & Design | 2022

Overview

做伙動, learning platform established for Chang Gung Health Culture Village which won the Symbol of National Quality mark in 2021, hoping to use this platform to increase activity participation and strong the physical and mental abilities of the senior citizens. And also reduce the burden of health carer, and provide health data tracking. After the proof of time, if it's work, it may be used in the society and provide the national senior citizens learning registration, indirectly make them strong, and reduce the disease pain.

My role

Competitive Analysis, User interview, Research, UI/UX Design (Linebot/Web), Promotional Video

Duration

September 2021 to May 2022 (9 months)

Teams

Designers (1), Design Professor (3), Engineer team (**), Cooperated Field (2)

User Problem

I categorizes users into villagers who participate in activities and health-carer who plan activities. The average age of the villagers is 70+, and the past platform lacks "sorting" feature and personal activities management feature. And also they are not good at network, it leads to bad user experience such as registration and searching difficulties. However, the health-carer needs to manage and plan activities. In the past, the whole data records on paper, which made it difficult to manage activities. Therefore, it's a burden to make health-carer analyze what new activities should be held or optimized difficultly.

The Goal

I’d like to create something that can solve user problem. It comes down to the following three aspects:

  • Useful: Users should be able to find information they need quickly
  • Suitable: Users should be able to find the information in familiarly way
  • Data-based: If users have more questions, they can reach out for the answer

The 3 Design Highlights You Should Know

  • Personalized Recommendations Help Make Decisions

  • The Right Platform Uses More Smoothly

  • Online Activity History Makes The Next Activity Better

User Interview

To Understand the Insight of Health Carer

This is a whole-new project. We must understand the working mode of the health village, current conditions, and internal administrative knowledge in order to plan effectively. Therefore, we decided to conduct user interviews, and the target is the staff of the activity department of the health village, so that we can collect a large of basic knowledge at the minimum cost.

Research Goal

  • Understanding current conditions and basic knowledge

Find-Outs

  1. Hard-to-Find Info: In most cases, paper records are used, such as: roll call, activity content, which often makes the information difficult to find and keep. Therefore, they often rely on rules of thumb to plan activities, and even help villagers to manually register for activities to add extra work pressure.
  2. The Wrong Platform: There are also some activities that use the online platform to register, but the most of villagers are not familiar with network, so they will ask staff to assist in registration. Some elders don’t even use the platform, so they call the counter directly and ask the staff to help them join the activity by using background. It makes the original convenience to become a burden instead.
  3. No Place to Remind: If there is a temporary difficulty in the activity to make the location or time should be changed, the news is announced on the official website. However, the villagers do not use the Internet frequently, there's something problems will happened, such as losing information and going to the wrong classroom.

Find-Outs

  1. Users want all information in one place: The activities of the health village are scattered on the course platform, official website, bulletin board and classroom door. When users are looking for new fun, they need a platform to integrate all the information.

    2. Users don't like fragmented messages

  2. Users want to use a familiar platform: Seniors tend to use a familiar platform, so they don't need to pay extra time to learn new knowledge.
  3. Users want a simple workflow: The reason is the seniors are not used to typing, easily get lost in the journey, and are not good at the Internet.
  4. Users want to be guided: When they lost in the platform, hoping that there is something that can guide them how to achieve their goal, instead of asking others for help many times.
  5. Users want to record activities: Activity need to be recorded to avoid forgetting. For the villagers, they hope someone can remind them to participate in the activity; For the staff, they hope to record the activity process of the senior for analysis.

    6. Users desire to record and be reminded

Vision

With all of Find-outs above, we run a design sprint. After that, we pinned down the design goals in the session:

  • Unified: Unify fragmented activity information across Health Village
  • Accessible: Guide them through their task goals
  • Individual: Provide different activities contents for individual villagers

  • Cannot be Integrated: This project will eventually be integrated with other projects into a LINE OA Line Official Account Can carry out brand marketing, functions with dialogue robots, designated group messages, CRM , but now the operation process of the two is different.

Solution

I reunderstand the resources at hand, LINE OA Line Official Account Can carry out brand marketing, functions with dialogue robots, designated group messages, CRM , and I conducted the competitor analysis again and discussed the results with the engineering team. Final, I got a positive answer.

I reunderstanding the user needs, stakeholders' questions, and I re-planned the overall flow, Here's some points:

  1. Integrate: Move the functions of the webpage to LINE OA
  2. Chatbot instead: We build dialogue scripts to guide the user's workflow to avoid limitations of LINE

FINAL DESIGN

We designed the UI of the front-stage, and the main users are those villagers. Therefore, I have studied the design specifications of the APP for the seniors, meeting with staff of health village to build dialogue scripts, and following with the Find-Outs above, I have come to the conclusions, Here's the following main points:

  • Clear: High contrast color, Big word, Big icon with name...
  • Bright & Energy: High saturation color, friendly text...
  • Easy-to-Understand: Intuitive text...

My first step is to set the brand color first, because it is an activity platform, I hope to have a sense of vitality, and then refer to the above main points to complete the UI design specifications, and finally we complete, Let's check it out!

Takeaway

The first whole project

Unlike designing a function of product, I need to understand the entire product journey, the health village, the seniors, the stakeholders, and even myself. That is to say, it's my graduation project and a business project at the same time. I have to make unique ideas on the premise of actual launch. Therefore, it can't be avoid that interesting ideas will be threw. My goal is to find a balance between uniqueness and consistency.

The challenges of designing at scale

In the past, I completed school projects by myself or in cooperation with classmates that as a designer. This time, I cooperated with a complete team. Unlike before, there are many constraints on cooperative design, such as: the brand concept of the customer, the persistence of stakeholders, the engineering team's limit. It takes a lot of time and effort to understand their requirements and reach alignment. There were also emergencies in the development, the product was redesigned, and it was a thrilling but perfect ending experience.
